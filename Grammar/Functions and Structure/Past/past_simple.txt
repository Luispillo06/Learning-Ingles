================================================================================
                              PAST SIMPLE
                         (Pasado Simple en Inglés)
================================================================================

El Past Simple se usa para hablar de acciones COMPLETADAS en el pasado,
en un momento específico.

================================================================================
                         STRUCTURE (Estructura)
================================================================================

AFFIRMATIVE (Afirmativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    VERB (past form)    +    COMPLEMENT                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/He/She/It/We/They    worked/went/saw    yesterday.                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I worked yesterday.
→ She visited Paris last year.
→ They went to the cinema.
→ He played football on Saturday.

NEGATIVE (Negativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    DID NOT (DIDN'T)    +    VERB (base form)                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/He/She/It/We/They    didn't    work/go/see    yesterday.             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I didn't work yesterday.
→ She didn't go to the party.
→ They didn't see the movie.
→ He didn't call me.

INTERROGATIVE (Interrogativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  DID    +    SUBJECT    +    VERB (base form)    +    ?                     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Did        I/you/he/she/it/we/they    work/go/see    yesterday?            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ Did you work yesterday?
→ Did she go to the party?
→ Did they see the movie?
→ Where did he go?

SHORT ANSWERS (Respuestas cortas):

→ Did you see him?         Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.
→ Did she call?            Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.

================================================================================
                    REGULAR VERBS: SPELLING RULES
                    (Verbos regulares: Reglas de ortografía)
================================================================================

GENERAL RULE: Add -ED
    work → worked           play → played
    clean → cleaned         watch → watched
    open → opened           listen → listened

VERBS ENDING IN -E: Add -D
    live → lived            dance → danced
    love → loved            arrive → arrived
    like → liked            use → used

VERBS ENDING IN CONSONANT + Y: Change Y to -IED
    study → studied         try → tried
    cry → cried             carry → carried
    worry → worried         hurry → hurried

VERBS ENDING IN VOWEL + Y: Add -ED
    play → played           stay → stayed
    enjoy → enjoyed         destroy → destroyed

ONE SYLLABLE CVC: Double final consonant + -ED
    stop → stopped          plan → planned
    drop → dropped          shop → shopped
    rob → robbed            beg → begged

TWO SYLLABLES (stress on last): Double final consonant
    prefer → preferred      admit → admitted
    occur → occurred        refer → referred

TWO SYLLABLES (stress NOT on last): Just add -ED
    visit → visited         open → opened
    listen → listened       happen → happened

================================================================================
                    THE VERB "TO BE" IN PAST
                    (El verbo "ser/estar" en pasado)
================================================================================

AFFIRMATIVE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  I/He/She/It         was         happy/tired/at home                        │
│  You/We/They         were        happy/tired/at home                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I was tired yesterday.
→ She was at school.
→ They were happy.
→ We were friends.

NEGATIVE:
→ I wasn't (was not) at home.
→ She wasn't happy.
→ They weren't (were not) ready.
→ We weren't there.

INTERROGATIVE:
→ Was I late?
→ Was she at the party?
→ Were they happy?
→ Where were you?

                         USES (Usos)
================================================================================

1. COMPLETED ACTIONS IN THE PAST (Acciones completadas):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I visited my grandmother last week.                                           │
│ Visité a mi abuela la semana pasada.                                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She bought a new car yesterday.                                               │
│ Ella compró un carro nuevo ayer.                                              │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ They moved to London in 2015.                                                 │
│ Ellos se mudaron a Londres en 2015.                                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ We finished the project on Friday.                                            │
│ Terminamos el proyecto el viernes.                                            │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. SERIES OF COMPLETED ACTIONS (Serie de acciones):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I woke up, had breakfast, and went to work.                                   │
│ Me desperté, desayuné y fui al trabajo.                                       │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She opened the door, walked in, and sat down.                                 │
│ Ella abrió la puerta, entró y se sentó.                                       │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ First, we arrived. Then, we checked in. Finally, we relaxed.                  │
│ Primero llegamos. Luego, hicimos check-in. Finalmente, nos relajamos.         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

3. PAST HABITS (Hábitos pasados) - also see USED TO:

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ When I was a child, I played in the park every day.                           │
│ Cuando era niño, jugaba en el parque todos los días.                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She always walked to school when she was young.                               │
│ Ella siempre caminaba a la escuela cuando era joven.                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

4. PAST STATES OR SITUATIONS (Estados pasados):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I lived in Paris for five years. (I don't live there now)                     │
│ Viví en París durante cinco años. (Ya no vivo allí)                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She was a doctor before she retired.                                          │
│ Ella era doctora antes de jubilarse.                                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ They owned a restaurant in the 90s.                                           │
│ Ellos tenían un restaurante en los 90.                                        │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    TIME EXPRESSIONS (Expresiones de tiempo)
================================================================================

YESTERDAY:
    yesterday                       → ayer
    yesterday morning/afternoon     → ayer por la mañana/tarde
    the day before yesterday        → anteayer

LAST:
    last night                      → anoche
    last week/month/year            → la semana/mes/año pasado
    last Monday/Tuesday...          → el lunes/martes pasado
    last summer/winter              → el verano/invierno pasado

AGO:
    two days ago                    → hace dos días
    a week ago                      → hace una semana
    three months ago                → hace tres meses
    a long time ago                 → hace mucho tiempo

IN + YEAR:
    in 2015                         → en 2015
    in the 1990s                    → en los años 90

OTHER:
    when I was young                → cuando era joven
    when I was a child              → cuando era niño
    at 5 o'clock                    → a las 5
    on Monday                       → el lunes
    this morning (if past)          → esta mañana
    the other day                   → el otro día

================================================================================
                    PAST SIMPLE vs PRESENT PERFECT
================================================================================

┌──────────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PAST SIMPLE                          │ PRESENT PERFECT                        │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Specific time in the past            │ No specific time                       │
│ I saw him yesterday.                 │ I have seen him.                       │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Finished time period                 │ Unfinished time period                 │
│ I ate breakfast this morning.        │ I have eaten breakfast today.          │
│ (morning is over)                    │ (day is not over)                      │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Completed situation                  │ Situation continues                    │
│ I lived in London for 5 years.       │ I have lived here for 5 years.         │
│ (I don't live there now)             │ (I still live here)                    │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ yesterday, ago, last, in 2015        │ ever, never, just, already, yet        │
│ when, at 5 o'clock                   │ for, since, today, this week           │
└──────────────────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘
