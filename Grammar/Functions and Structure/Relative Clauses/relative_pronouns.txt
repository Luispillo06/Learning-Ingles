                    RELATIVE PRONOUNS
              (Pronombres Relativos en Detalle)
================================================================================

================================================================================
                    WHO vs WHOM
================================================================================

WHO = Subject (quién hace la acción)
WHOM = Object (quién recibe la acción) - formal

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ WHO (subject):                                                                │
│ The man WHO called you is here.                                               │
│ (Who called? → the man = subject)                                             │
│ El hombre que te llamó está aquí.                                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The woman WHO works here is my friend.                                        │
│ (Who works? → the woman = subject)                                            │
│ La mujer que trabaja aquí es mi amiga.                                        │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ WHOM (object - formal):                                                       │
│ The man WHOM you called is here.                                              │
│ (You called whom? → the man = object)                                         │
│ El hombre a quien llamaste está aquí.                                         │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The woman WHOM I met is my friend.                                            │
│ (I met whom? → the woman = object)                                            │
│ La mujer a quien conocí es mi amiga.                                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

INFORMAL: WHO can replace WHOM (or omit completely):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FORMAL:   The person whom I spoke to...                                       │
│ INFORMAL: The person who I spoke to...                                        │
│ INFORMAL: The person I spoke to... (pronoun omitted)                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHOM after PREPOSITIONS (formal):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The person to whom I spoke was helpful.                                       │
│ La persona con quien hablé fue servicial.                                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The colleague with whom I work is nice.                                       │
│ El colega con quien trabajo es simpático.                                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The man for whom she works is strict.                                         │
│ El hombre para quien ella trabaja es estricto.                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHICH vs THAT
================================================================================

WHICH = things and animals
THAT = things, animals, AND people (only in defining clauses)

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ BOTH OK in DEFINING clauses:                                                  │
│ The book which/that I read was good.                                          │
│ The car which/that he drives is fast.                                         │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ONLY WHICH in NON-DEFINING clauses:                                           │
│ My car, which is new, broke down.  ✓                                          │
│ My car, that is new, broke down.   ✗                                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

PREFER THAT after:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SUPERLATIVES:     the best/worst/biggest... that                              │
│                   It's the best movie that I've ever seen.                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ORDINALS:         the first/last/only... that                                 │
│                   She was the first that arrived.                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ALL/EVERYTHING:   All that glitters is not gold.                              │
│                   Everything that he said was true.                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ NOTHING/SOMETHING: There's nothing that I can do.                             │
│                    Is there something that you need?                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ PEOPLE + THINGS:  The people and things that I love...                        │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHOSE
================================================================================

WHOSE = possession (cuyo/a, de quien)
Can be used for PEOPLE and THINGS.

FOR PEOPLE:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The man whose car was stolen...                                               │
│ El hombre cuyo coche fue robado...                                            │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ A woman whose children go to my school...                                     │
│ Una mujer cuyos hijos van a mi colegio...                                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The author whose books I love...                                              │
│ El autor cuyos libros me encantan...                                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ People whose jobs are stressful...                                            │
│ Las personas cuyos trabajos son estresantes...                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

FOR THINGS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The house whose roof is red...                                                │
│ La casa cuyo tejado es rojo...                                                │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ A company whose profits are increasing...                                     │
│ Una empresa cuyos beneficios están aumentando...                              │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The book whose cover is damaged...                                            │
│ El libro cuya portada está dañada...                                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

STRUCTURE: WHOSE + NOUN (always followed by a noun)
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The man whose CAR was stolen...                                               │
│ A woman whose CHILDREN go to school...                                        │
│ The house whose ROOF is red...                                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

ALTERNATIVES TO WHOSE (more informal):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ WHOSE:       The man whose car was stolen...                                  │
│ ALTERNATIVE: The man with the stolen car... (less precise)                    │
│ ALTERNATIVE: The man that had his car stolen... (different structure)         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHERE, WHEN, WHY
================================================================================

WHERE = in/at which (for places)
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The hotel where we stayed = The hotel at which we stayed                      │
│ The city where I was born = The city in which I was born                      │
│ The office where I work = The office at which I work                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ This is the place where I first met her.                                      │
│ Este es el lugar donde la conocí por primera vez.                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I know a restaurant where they serve great food.                              │
│ Conozco un restaurante donde sirven comida genial.                            │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHEN = on/in which (for times)
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The day when I was born = The day on which I was born                         │
│ The year when it happened = The year in which it happened                     │
│ The moment when he arrived = The moment at which he arrived                   │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I remember the summer when we first met.                                      │
│ Recuerdo el verano en que nos conocimos.                                      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ That was the year when everything changed.                                    │
│ Ese fue el año en que todo cambió.                                            │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHY = for which (only after "reason")
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The reason why I left = The reason for which I left                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ That's the reason why I'm here.                                               │
│ Esa es la razón por la que estoy aquí.                                        │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I don't understand the reason why he's angry.                                 │
│ No entiendo la razón por la que está enfadado.                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NOTE: WHERE, WHEN, WHY can often be omitted:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The hotel (where) we stayed...                                                │
│ The day (when) I met her...                                                   │
│ The reason (why) I called...                                                  │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    PREPOSITIONS WITH RELATIVE PRONOUNS
================================================================================

FORMAL: Preposition + whom/which
INFORMAL: Preposition at the end

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FORMAL:   The person to whom I spoke was helpful.                             │
│ INFORMAL: The person (who/that) I spoke to was helpful.                       │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ FORMAL:   The chair on which I sat was broken.                                │
│ INFORMAL: The chair (which/that) I sat on was broken.                         │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ FORMAL:   The company for which she works is big.                             │
│ INFORMAL: The company (which/that) she works for is big.                      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ FORMAL:   The man with whom I traveled was nice.                              │
│ INFORMAL: The man (who/that) I traveled with was nice.                        │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

⚠️ CANNOT use THAT after prepositions:
   ❌ The person to that I spoke...
   ✓  The person to whom I spoke...
   ✓  The person that I spoke to...
