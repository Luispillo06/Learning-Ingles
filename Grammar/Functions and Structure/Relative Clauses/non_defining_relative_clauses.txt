================================================================================
                  NON-DEFINING RELATIVE CLAUSES
               (Oraciones de Relativo Explicativas)
================================================================================

NON-DEFINING RELATIVE CLAUSES give EXTRA information.
Without them, we still know who or what we're talking about.
They are like a PARENTHESIS - additional, not essential.

================================================================================
                    CHARACTERISTICS
================================================================================

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ✓ WITH COMMAS (before and after the clause)                                 │
│ ✓ Information is EXTRA (not needed to identify)                             │
│ ✗ CANNOT use THAT                                                           │
│ ✗ CANNOT omit the relative pronoun                                          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    DEFINING vs NON-DEFINING
================================================================================

COMPARE:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DEFINING (no commas - essential):                                             │
│ My brother who lives in London is a doctor.                                   │
│ → I have MORE than one brother. Which one? The one in London.                 │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ NON-DEFINING (with commas - extra info):                                      │
│ My brother, who lives in London, is a doctor.                                 │
│ → I have ONLY ONE brother. Extra info: he lives in London.                    │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DEFINING:                                                                     │
│ The students who passed the exam are happy.                                   │
│ → Only SOME students passed (not all of them).                                │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ NON-DEFINING:                                                                 │
│ The students, who passed the exam, are happy.                                 │
│ → ALL the students passed the exam.                                           │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WITH WHO (for people)
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ My mother, who is 65, still works.                                            │
│ Mi madre, que tiene 65 años, todavía trabaja.                                 │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Barack Obama, who was the 44th US president, wrote a book.                    │
│ Barack Obama, que fue el 44º presidente de EEUU, escribió un libro.           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ My neighbor, who is very friendly, helped me move.                            │
│ Mi vecino, que es muy amable, me ayudó a mudarme.                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ John, who I've known for years, got married.                                  │
│ John, a quien conozco desde hace años, se casó.                               │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The teacher, who everyone respects, is retiring.                              │
│ El profesor, a quien todos respetan, se jubila.                               │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

⚠️ CANNOT use THAT:
  ❌ My mother, that is 65, still works.
  ✓  My mother, who is 65, still works.

================================================================================
                    WITH WHICH (for things/animals)
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ My car, which I bought last year, has broken down.                            │
│ Mi coche, que compré el año pasado, se ha averiado.                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The Eiffel Tower, which was built in 1889, is in Paris.                       │
│ La Torre Eiffel, que fue construida en 1889, está en París.                   │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ His latest book, which I've just finished, is excellent.                      │
│ Su último libro, que acabo de terminar, es excelente.                         │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ My dog, which is very old, sleeps all day.                                    │
│ Mi perro, que es muy viejo, duerme todo el día.                               │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The Amazon, which is the largest river, flows through Brazil.                 │
│ El Amazonas, que es el río más grande, fluye por Brasil.                      │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

⚠️ CANNOT use THAT:
  ❌ My car, that I bought last year, has broken down.
  ✓  My car, which I bought last year, has broken down.

================================================================================
                    WITH WHOSE (possession)
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ My neighbor, whose son is a doctor, is very proud.                            │
│ Mi vecino, cuyo hijo es médico, está muy orgulloso.                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Shakespeare, whose plays are famous, was born in Stratford.                   │
│ Shakespeare, cuyas obras son famosas, nació en Stratford.                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ My boss, whose office is next to mine, is very strict.                        │
│ Mi jefe, cuya oficina está junto a la mía, es muy estricto.                   │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WITH WHERE (places)
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Paris, where I was born, is a beautiful city.                                 │
│ París, donde nací, es una ciudad preciosa.                                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The Louvre, where the Mona Lisa is displayed, is in Paris.                    │
│ El Louvre, donde se exhibe la Mona Lisa, está en París.                       │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ My hometown, where I grew up, has changed a lot.                              │
│ Mi ciudad natal, donde crecí, ha cambiado mucho.                              │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WITH WHEN (times)
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1969, when man landed on the moon, was a historic year.                       │
│ 1969, cuando el hombre pisó la luna, fue un año histórico.                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Christmas, when families get together, is my favorite holiday.                │
│ Navidad, cuando las familias se reúnen, es mi fiesta favorita.                │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The 1990s, when I was young, were great times.                                │
│ Los años 90, cuando yo era joven, fueron grandes tiempos.                     │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHICH REFERRING TO A WHOLE CLAUSE
================================================================================

WHICH can refer to the entire previous idea (not just a noun):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ He passed the exam, which surprised everyone.                                 │
│ Aprobó el examen, lo cual sorprendió a todos.                                 │
│ (which = the fact that he passed)                                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She arrived late, which made us angry.                                        │
│ Llegó tarde, lo cual nos enfadó.                                              │
│ (which = the fact that she arrived late)                                      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ He didn't call, which worried me.                                             │
│ No llamó, lo cual me preocupó.                                                │
│ (which = the fact that he didn't call)                                        │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ They won the game, which was unexpected.                                      │
│ Ganaron el partido, lo cual fue inesperado.                                   │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She speaks five languages, which is impressive.                               │
│ Habla cinco idiomas, lo cual es impresionante.                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NOTE: Only WHICH can do this (not THAT).

================================================================================
                    WITH PROPER NOUNS
================================================================================

Proper nouns (names of specific people, places, things) ALWAYS need 
non-defining clauses because they are already identified:

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ London, which is the capital of England, is very expensive.                   │
│ Londres, que es la capital de Inglaterra, es muy cara.                        │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Mount Everest, which is in Nepal, is the highest mountain.                    │
│ El Monte Everest, que está en Nepal, es la montaña más alta.                  │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Albert Einstein, who developed the theory of relativity, was German.          │
│ Albert Einstein, que desarrolló la teoría de la relatividad, era alemán.      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The Amazon River, which flows through Brazil, is very long.                   │
│ El río Amazonas, que fluye por Brasil, es muy largo.                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    PREPOSITIONS IN NON-DEFINING CLAUSES
================================================================================

FORMAL (preposition before pronoun):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ My teacher, to whom I owe a lot, retired last year.                           │
│ Mi profesor, a quien le debo mucho, se jubiló el año pasado.                  │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The company, for which he works, is very successful.                          │
│ La empresa, para la cual trabaja, tiene mucho éxito.                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

INFORMAL (preposition at the end):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ My teacher, who I owe a lot to, retired last year.                            │
│ The company, which he works for, is very successful.                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                           QUICK SUMMARY
================================================================================

┌──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                  NON-DEFINING RELATIVE CLAUSES                               │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ • WITH COMMAS (always)                                                       │
│ • EXTRA information (can be removed)                                         │
│ • CANNOT use THAT                                                            │
│ • CANNOT omit the pronoun                                                    │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ My brother, who lives in Madrid, is a doctor.                                │
│ The Eiffel Tower, which is in Paris, was built in 1889.                      │
│ My boss, whose car is a BMW, is very rich.                                   │
│ Paris, where I was born, is beautiful.                                       │
│ 1969, when man walked on the moon, was historic.                             │
│ He failed, which was surprising. (which = whole clause)                      │
├──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Use with proper nouns: London, Einstein, The Amazon...                       │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
