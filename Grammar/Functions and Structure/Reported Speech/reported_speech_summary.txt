================================================================================
                    TENSE CHANGES (BACKSHIFT)
================================================================================

┌────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DIRECT SPEECH              │ REPORTED SPEECH                                  │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Simple             │ Past Simple                                      │
│ "I work"                   │ He said he worked                                │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Continuous         │ Past Continuous                                  │
│ "I am working"             │ He said he was working                           │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Perfect            │ Past Perfect                                     │
│ "I have worked"            │ He said he had worked                            │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Past Simple                │ Past Perfect                                     │
│ "I worked"                 │ He said he had worked                            │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Past Continuous            │ Past Perfect Continuous                          │
│ "I was working"            │ He said he had been working                      │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ will                       │ would                                            │
│ can                        │ could                                            │
│ may                        │ might                                            │
│ must                       │ had to                                           │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ could, would, should       │ NO CHANGE                                        │
│ might, ought to            │ NO CHANGE                                        │
│ Past Perfect               │ NO CHANGE                                        │
└────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    THREE TYPES OF REPORTED SPEECH
================================================================================

1. STATEMENTS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRUCTURE: Subject + said/told + (that) + clause                              │
│                                                                               │
│ "I am tired."  →  She said (that) she was tired.                              │
│ "I have finished."  →  He said (that) he had finished.                        │
│ "I will come."  →  She said (that) she would come.                            │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. QUESTIONS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ YES/NO: Subject + asked + if/whether + subject + verb                         │
│                                                                               │
│ "Are you coming?"  →  He asked if/whether I was coming.                       │
│ "Did you see it?"  →  She asked if/whether I had seen it.                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ WH-QUESTIONS: Subject + asked + wh-word + subject + verb                      │
│                                                                               │
│ "Where do you live?"  →  He asked where I lived.                              │
│ "What time is it?"  →  She asked what time it was.                            │
│                                                                               │
│ ⚠️ Use STATEMENT word order (not question order)                              │
│ ❌ He asked where did I live.                                                 │
│ ✓  He asked where I lived.                                                    │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

3. COMMANDS/REQUESTS/ADVICE:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STRUCTURE: Verb + person + (not) to + infinitive                              │
│                                                                               │
│ "Wait here."  →  She told me to wait there.                                   │
│ "Don't go."  →  He told me not to go.                                         │
│ "Please help me."  →  She asked me to help her.                               │
│ "You should rest."  →  He advised me to rest.                                 │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    SAY vs TELL
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SAY:  No person required                                                      │
│       She said (that) she was happy.                                          │
│       She said to me (that) she was happy.                                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TELL: Always needs a person                                                   │
│       She told ME (that) she was happy.                                       │
│       ❌ She told that she was happy. (WRONG!)                                │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    REPORTING VERBS SUMMARY
================================================================================

STATEMENTS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ say, tell, explain, mention, admit, deny, claim, complain, announce,          │
│ add, reply, answer, state, report, promise, agree                             │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

QUESTIONS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ask, want to know, wonder, inquire                                            │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

COMMANDS/REQUESTS/ADVICE (+ person + to infinitive):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ tell, ask, order, command, advise, warn, encourage, urge, beg, remind,        │
│ invite, instruct, forbid                                                      │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

SPECIAL PATTERNS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ offer + to infinitive:    He offered to help.                                 │
│ promise + to infinitive:  She promised to come.                               │
│ refuse + to infinitive:   He refused to pay.                                  │
│ agree + to infinitive:    She agreed to wait.                                 │
│ threaten + to infinitive: He threatened to leave.                             │
│ deny + gerund:            She denied stealing it.                             │
│ suggest + gerund:         He suggested going out.                             │
│ suggest + (that) + subjunctive: She suggested (that) we go.                   │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHEN NOT TO BACKSHIFT
================================================================================

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. Reporting verb in PRESENT tense:                                           │
│    "I am busy."  →  She says she is busy.                                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 2. GENERAL TRUTHS / FACTS:                                                    │
│    "The Earth is round."  →  He said the Earth is round.                      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 3. STILL TRUE at reporting time:                                              │
│    "I love you."  →  He said he loves me. (still loves me)                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 4. IMMEDIATE reporting (same day):                                            │
│    "I'll be there at 5."  →  She said she'll be here at 5.                    │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    COMMON MISTAKES
================================================================================

  ❌ WRONG                              ✓ CORRECT
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  She told that she was tired.          She said (that) she was tired.
                                        She told me (that) she was tired.
  
  He asked where did I live.            He asked where I lived.
  
  She asked if was I coming.            She asked if I was coming.
  
  He said me to wait.                   He told me to wait.
  
  She suggested me to go.               She suggested going.
                                        She suggested that I go.
  
  He asked that I was happy.            He asked if I was happy.

================================================================================
                    TRANSFORMATION PRACTICE
================================================================================

COMPLETE EXAMPLE:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DIRECT (Monday, in London):                                                   │
│ "I saw your brother yesterday and I'm meeting him again tomorrow.             │
│ Please don't tell anyone about this. Where do you work now?                   │
│ You should visit us here next week."                                          │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ REPORTED (Wednesday, in Madrid):                                              │
│ She said she had seen my brother the day before (Sunday) and she was          │
│ meeting him again the following day (Tuesday). She asked me not to tell       │
│ anyone about that. She asked where I worked then. She advised me to           │
│ visit them there the following week.                                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
