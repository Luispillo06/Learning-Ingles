================================================================================
                       REPORTED SPEECH
                      (Estilo Indirecto)
================================================================================

REPORTED SPEECH (también llamado INDIRECT SPEECH) se usa para contar
lo que otra persona dijo, sin usar sus palabras exactas.

================================================================================
                    DIRECT vs REPORTED SPEECH
================================================================================

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DIRECT SPEECH (palabras exactas):                                           │
│ She said, "I am tired."                                                     │
│ Ella dijo: "Estoy cansada."                                                 │
│                                                                             │
│ REPORTED SPEECH (contando lo que dijo):                                     │
│ She said (that) she was tired.                                              │
│ Ella dijo que estaba cansada.                                               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    BASIC STRUCTURE
================================================================================

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  REPORTING VERB    +    (THAT)    +    REPORTED CLAUSE                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  She said              (that)         she was tired.                        │
│  He told me            (that)         he would come.                        │
│  They explained        (that)         they had finished.                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NOTE: "That" is optional and often omitted in spoken English.

================================================================================
                    SAY vs TELL
================================================================================

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SAY:  No need for a person after it                                         │
│       She said (that) she was happy.                                        │
│       She said TO ME (that) she was happy.                                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ TELL: Always needs a person after it                                        │
│       She told ME (that) she was happy.                                     │
│       ❌ She told that she was happy. (WRONG!)                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

COMMON REPORTING VERBS:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ say, tell, explain, mention, admit, deny, claim, promise, agree, refuse,      │
│ suggest, complain, warn, announce, add, reply, answer, report, state          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    TENSE CHANGES (BACKSHIFT)
================================================================================

When the reporting verb is in the PAST (said, told), tenses usually
shift back one step (BACKSHIFT):

┌────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DIRECT SPEECH              │ REPORTED SPEECH                                  │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Simple             │ Past Simple                                      │
│ "I work here."             │ He said he worked there.                         │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Continuous         │ Past Continuous                                  │
│ "I am working."            │ He said he was working.                          │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Present Perfect            │ Past Perfect                                     │
│ "I have finished."         │ He said he had finished.                         │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Past Simple                │ Past Perfect                                     │
│ "I worked yesterday."      │ He said he had worked the day before.            │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Past Continuous            │ Past Perfect Continuous                          │
│ "I was working."           │ He said he had been working.                     │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Will                       │ Would                                            │
│ "I will help you."         │ He said he would help me.                        │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Can                        │ Could                                            │
│ "I can swim."              │ He said he could swim.                           │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ May                        │ Might                                            │
│ "I may come."              │ He said he might come.                           │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Must                       │ Had to / Must                                    │
│ "I must go."               │ He said he had to go.                            │
└────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NO CHANGE NEEDED:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Past Perfect    → Past Perfect (no change)                                    │
│ "I had seen it."  He said he had seen it.                                     │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Would, could, should, might, ought to → NO CHANGE                             │
│ "I would help."   He said he would help.                                      │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    WHEN NOT TO BACKSHIFT
================================================================================

You DON'T need to change tenses when:

1. THE REPORTING VERB IS PRESENT:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "I am busy."  →  She says she is busy. (no change)                            │
│ "I will call."  →  He says he will call. (no change)                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. IT'S STILL TRUE (general truths/facts):
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "The Earth is round."  →  She said the Earth is round. (still true)           │
│ "Water boils at 100°C."  →  He said water boils at 100°C.                     │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

3. IT'S STILL RELEVANT:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "I love you."  →  He said he loves me. (still loves me now)                   │
│ "I'm 25 years old."  →  She said she's 25. (still 25)                         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    PRONOUN CHANGES
================================================================================

Pronouns change to match the new speaker's perspective:

┌────────────────────────────┬──────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ DIRECT                     │ REPORTED                                         │
├────────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I                          │ he / she                                         │
│ me                         │ him / her                                        │
│ my                         │ his / her                                        │
│ we                         │ they                                             │
│ us                         │ them                                             │
│ our                        │ their                                            │
│ you (to me)                │ I / me                                           │
│ your (to me)               │ my                                               │
└────────────────────────────┴──────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

EXAMPLES:
┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "I love my job."                                                              │
│ She said she loved her job.                                                   │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ "We will help you."                                                           │
│ They said they would help me.                                                 │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ "You are my best friend."                                                     │
│ He told me I was his best friend.                                             │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
