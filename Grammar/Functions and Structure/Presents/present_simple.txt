================================================================================
                            PRESENT SIMPLE
                         (Presente Simple en Inglés)
================================================================================

El Present Simple se usa para hablar de hechos, rutinas, hábitos y 
verdades generales.

================================================================================
                         STRUCTURE (Estructura)
================================================================================

AFFIRMATIVE (Afirmativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    VERB (base form)    +    COMPLEMENT                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/We/They    work               every day.                             │
│  He/She/It        works              every day.                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I work in an office.
→ She works in a hospital.
→ They live in London.
→ He plays football on Saturdays.

NEGATIVE (Negativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    DO/DOES + NOT    +    VERB (base form)                     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/We/They    don't (do not)      work    on Sundays.                   │
│  He/She/It        doesn't (does not)  work    on Sundays.                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I don't like coffee.
→ She doesn't eat meat.
→ They don't speak French.
→ He doesn't live here.

INTERROGATIVE (Interrogativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  DO/DOES    +    SUBJECT    +    VERB (base form)    +    ?                 │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Do         I/you/we/they        work               here?                   │
│  Does       he/she/it            work               here?                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ Do you like pizza?
→ Does she speak English?
→ Do they live in Madrid?
→ Does he work here?

SHORT ANSWERS (Respuestas cortas):

→ Do you like coffee?      Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
→ Does she work here?      Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.

================================================================================
                    SPELLING RULES FOR HE/SHE/IT
                    (Reglas de ortografía para 3ª persona)
================================================================================

GENERAL RULE: Add -S
    work → works            play → plays
    like → likes            read → reads
    live → lives            eat → eats

VERBS ENDING IN -S, -SS, -SH, -CH, -X, -O: Add -ES
    pass → passes           wash → washes
    watch → watches         fix → fixes
    go → goes               do → does

VERBS ENDING IN CONSONANT + Y: Change Y to -IES
    study → studies         try → tries
    fly → flies             carry → carries
    cry → cries             worry → worries

VERBS ENDING IN VOWEL + Y: Add -S
    play → plays            stay → stays
    enjoy → enjoys          buy → buys

IRREGULAR:
    have → has
    be → is

================================================================================
                    TIME EXPRESSIONS (Expresiones de tiempo)
================================================================================

FREQUENCY ADVERBS (Adverbios de frecuencia):

always          → siempre          (100%)
usually         → usualmente       (80%)
often           → a menudo         (70%)
sometimes       → a veces          (50%)
occasionally    → ocasionalmente   (30%)
rarely          → raramente        (10%)
hardly ever     → casi nunca       (5%)
never           → nunca            (0%)

POSITION: Before the main verb, after BE

→ She always arrives on time.
→ I usually have breakfast at 8.
→ He is always late. (after BE)
→ They never go to the cinema.

OTHER TIME EXPRESSIONS:

every day/week/month/year       → cada día/semana/mes/año
every morning/afternoon/evening → cada mañana/tarde/noche
on Mondays/Tuesdays...          → los lunes/martes...
once a week/month               → una vez a la semana/mes
twice a week/month              → dos veces a la semana/mes
three times a week              → tres veces a la semana
in the morning/afternoon        → por la mañana/tarde
at night                        → por la noche
at the weekend                  → el fin de semana

→ I go to the gym every day.
→ She visits her parents once a week.
→ They play football on Saturdays.

================================================================================
                    STATIVE VERBS (Verbos de estado)
================================================================================

Estos verbos normalmente NO se usan en tiempos continuos.
Se usan en Present Simple incluso para situaciones actuales:

MENTAL STATES (Estados mentales):
know, believe, think, understand, remember, forget, realize,
recognize, suppose, imagine, doubt, mean

EMOTIONS (Emociones):
love, like, hate, dislike, want, need, prefer, wish

SENSES (Sentidos):
see, hear, smell, taste, feel, sound, look, seem, appear

POSSESSION (Posesión):
have, own, belong, possess, contain, consist

OTHER:
be, cost, weigh, measure, owe, deserve, fit

EJEMPLOS:
→ I know the answer. (NOT: I am knowing)
→ She loves chocolate. (NOT: She is loving)
→ This book belongs to me. (NOT: This book is belonging)
→ It costs $10. (NOT: It is costing)

                    PRESENT SIMPLE vs PRESENT CONTINUOUS
================================================================================

┌──────────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PRESENT SIMPLE                       │ PRESENT CONTINUOUS                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Habits, routines                     │ Actions happening NOW                  │
│ I drink coffee every morning.        │ I'm drinking coffee right now.         │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Permanent situations                 │ Temporary situations                   │
│ I live in Madrid.                    │ I'm living with my parents.            │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Facts and truths                     │ Changing situations                    │
│ Water boils at 100°C.                │ The weather is getting colder.         │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Scheduled events                     │ Future arrangements                    │
│ The train leaves at 9.               │ I'm meeting John tomorrow.             │
└──────────────────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘
