================================================================================
                           PRESENT PERFECT
                      (Presente Perfecto en Inglés)
================================================================================

El Present Perfect conecta el PASADO con el PRESENTE. Se usa para hablar
de experiencias, acciones recientes o situaciones que empezaron en el pasado
y continúan ahora.

================================================================================
                         STRUCTURE (Estructura)
================================================================================

AFFIRMATIVE (Afirmativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    HAVE/HAS    +    PAST PARTICIPLE (V3)    +    COMPLEMENT   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/We/They    have           worked/eaten/seen                          │
│  He/She/It        has            worked/eaten/seen                          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I have finished my homework.
→ She has lived here for 10 years.
→ They have seen that movie.
→ He has eaten breakfast.

CONTRACTIONS:
I have = I've                 He has = He's
She has = She's               It has = It's
We have = We've               You have = You've
They have = They've

NEGATIVE (Negativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    HAVE/HAS + NOT    +    PAST PARTICIPLE (V3)                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I/You/We/They    haven't (have not)     finished                           │
│  He/She/It        hasn't (has not)       finished                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I haven't seen him today.
→ She hasn't finished yet.
→ They haven't arrived.
→ He hasn't called me.

INTERROGATIVE (Interrogativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  HAVE/HAS    +    SUBJECT    +    PAST PARTICIPLE (V3)    +    ?            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Have             I/you/we/they      finished?                              │
│  Has              he/she/it          finished?                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ Have you eaten lunch?
→ Has she called you?
→ Have they arrived yet?
→ Where have you been?

SHORT ANSWERS (Respuestas cortas):

→ Have you finished?       Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
→ Has she arrived?         Yes, she has. / No, she hasn't.

================================================================================
                    PAST PARTICIPLE FORMS (V3)
                    (Formas del Participio Pasado)
================================================================================

REGULAR VERBS: Add -ED (same as Past Simple)
    work → worked           play → played
    finish → finished       study → studied
    live → lived            watch → watched

IRREGULAR VERBS: Must be memorized

================================================================================
                         USES (Usos)
================================================================================

1. LIFE EXPERIENCES (Experiencias de vida):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I have visited Paris three times.                                             │
│ He visitado París tres veces.                                                 │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She has never eaten sushi.                                                    │
│ Ella nunca ha comido sushi.                                                   │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Have you ever been to Japan?                                                  │
│ ¿Alguna vez has estado en Japón?                                              │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I have met many famous people.                                                │
│ He conocido a muchas personas famosas.                                        │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Keywords: ever, never, before, in my life

2. RECENT ACTIONS WITH PRESENT RESULTS (Acciones recientes con resultado presente):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I have lost my keys. (I can't find them now)                                  │
│ He perdido mis llaves. (No las encuentro ahora)                               │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She has broken her leg. (It's still broken)                                   │
│ Ella se ha roto la pierna. (Todavía está rota)                                │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I've just finished my homework. (It's done now)                               │
│ Acabo de terminar mi tarea. (Está hecha ahora)                                │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The taxi has arrived. (It's here now)                                         │
│ El taxi ha llegado. (Está aquí ahora)                                         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Keywords: just, already, yet

3. SITUATIONS THAT STARTED IN THE PAST AND CONTINUE NOW (FOR/SINCE):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I have lived here for ten years.                                              │
│ He vivido aquí durante diez años. (y sigo viviendo)                           │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She has worked here since 2015.                                               │
│ Ella ha trabajado aquí desde 2015. (y sigue trabajando)                       │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ We have known each other for 20 years.                                        │
│ Nos conocemos desde hace 20 años.                                             │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ He has been a teacher since he graduated.                                     │
│ Ha sido profesor desde que se graduó.                                         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

4. UNFINISHED TIME PERIODS (Períodos de tiempo sin terminar):

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ I have seen three movies this week.                                           │
│ He visto tres películas esta semana. (la semana no ha terminado)              │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She has called me twice today.                                                │
│ Ella me ha llamado dos veces hoy. (el día no ha terminado)                    │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I haven't eaten anything today.                                               │
│ No he comido nada hoy.                                                        │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Have you read the news this morning?                                          │
│ ¿Has leído las noticias esta mañana?                                          │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

Keywords: today, this week/month/year, this morning

================================================================================
                    KEY WORDS AND TIME EXPRESSIONS
================================================================================

FOR vs SINCE:

┌───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ FOR + DURATION (período de tiempo)     │ SINCE + POINT IN TIME (momento)      │
├───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ for two hours                          │ since 2 o'clock                      │
│ for three days                         │ since Monday                         │
│ for a week                             │ since last week                      │
│ for six months                         │ since January                        │
│ for ten years                          │ since 2015                           │
│ for a long time                        │ since I was a child                  │
│ for ages                               │ since we met                         │
└───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

JUST, ALREADY, YET:

JUST = acabar de (recién)
→ I have just finished. (Acabo de terminar)
→ She has just arrived. (Ella acaba de llegar)
Position: have/has + JUST + past participle

ALREADY = ya (antes de lo esperado)
→ I have already done it. (Ya lo he hecho)
→ She has already left. (Ella ya se ha ido)
Position: have/has + ALREADY + past participle

YET = todavía/aún (en negativas e interrogativas)
→ I haven't finished yet. (Todavía no he terminado)
→ Has she arrived yet? (¿Ha llegado ya?)
Position: at the END of the sentence

EVER, NEVER:

EVER = alguna vez (en preguntas)
→ Have you ever been to London? (¿Alguna vez has estado en Londres?)
→ Has she ever tried sushi? (¿Alguna vez ha probado sushi?)

NEVER = nunca
→ I have never seen snow. (Nunca he visto nieve)
→ She has never been abroad. (Ella nunca ha estado en el extranjero)

Position: have/has + EVER/NEVER + past participle

================================================================================
                    PRESENT PERFECT vs PAST SIMPLE
================================================================================

┌──────────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PRESENT PERFECT                      │ PAST SIMPLE                            │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ No specific time                     │ Specific time in the past              │
│ I have visited Paris.                │ I visited Paris in 2019.               │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Unfinished time period               │ Finished time period                   │
│ I have seen him today.               │ I saw him yesterday.                   │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Situation continues to now           │ Situation is finished                  │
│ I have lived here for 5 years.       │ I lived in London for 5 years.         │
│ (I still live here)                  │ (I don't live there now)               │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Result is important now              │ The action is important                │
│ I've lost my keys. (where are they?) │ I lost my keys. (what happened)        │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ ever, never, just, already, yet      │ yesterday, last week, ago, in 2015     │
│ for, since, today, this week         │ when I was young, at 5 o'clock         │
└──────────────────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

EJEMPLOS COMPARATIVOS:

→ I have seen that movie. (experiencia general)
→ I saw that movie last night. (momento específico)

→ She has lived in Paris. (experiencia en su vida)
→ She lived in Paris for 2 years. (período terminado)

→ Have you eaten? (¿Ya comiste? - resultado importa)
→ Did you eat? / When did you eat? (preguntando por la acción)

================================================================================
                    BEEN vs GONE
================================================================================

BEEN = ha ido y ha vuelto (fue y regresó)
→ She has been to Paris. (Ella ha estado en París - ya regresó)
→ I have been to the supermarket. (Fui al súper y volví)

GONE = ha ido y no ha vuelto (todavía está allí)
→ She has gone to Paris. (Ella ha ido a París - está allá ahora)
→ He has gone to work. (Él se fue a trabajar - está en el trabajo)
