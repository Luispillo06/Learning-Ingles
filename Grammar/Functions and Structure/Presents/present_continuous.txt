================================================================================
                          PRESENT CONTINUOUS
                      (Presente Continuo en Inglés)
================================================================================

El Present Continuous (también llamado Present Progressive) se usa para 
hablar de acciones que están ocurriendo AHORA o en este período de tiempo.

================================================================================
                         STRUCTURE (Estructura)
================================================================================

AFFIRMATIVE (Afirmativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    BE (am/is/are)    +    VERB-ING    +    COMPLEMENT         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I              am                     working          now.                │
│  He/She/It      is                     working          now.                │
│  You/We/They    are                    working          now.                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I am working right now.
→ She is cooking dinner.
→ They are watching TV.
→ He is studying English.

CONTRACTIONS (Contracciones):
I am = I'm                    He is = He's
She is = She's                It is = It's
We are = We're                You are = You're
They are = They're

NEGATIVE (Negativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  SUBJECT    +    BE + NOT    +    VERB-ING                                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  I              am not (I'm not)       working         now.                 │
│  He/She/It      is not (isn't)         working         now.                 │
│  You/We/They    are not (aren't)       working         now.                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ I'm not working today.
→ She isn't sleeping.
→ They aren't listening.
→ He isn't coming to the party.

INTERROGATIVE (Interrogativo):

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  BE (am/is/are)    +    SUBJECT    +    VERB-ING    +    ?                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Am                     I                working?                           │
│  Is                     he/she/it        working?                           │
│  Are                    you/we/they      working?                           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

→ Are you listening to me?
→ Is she working today?
→ Are they coming to the party?
→ What are you doing?

SHORT ANSWERS (Respuestas cortas):

→ Are you studying?        Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
→ Is she working?          Yes, she is. / No, she isn't.
→ Are they sleeping?       Yes, they are. / No, they aren't.

================================================================================
                    SPELLING RULES FOR -ING
                    (Reglas de ortografía para -ING)
================================================================================

GENERAL RULE: Add -ING
    work → working          read → reading
    play → playing          watch → watching
    eat → eating            sleep → sleeping

VERBS ENDING IN -E: Drop -E, add -ING
    make → making           write → writing
    live → living           dance → dancing
    come → coming           take → taking

VERBS ENDING IN -IE: Change -IE to -Y, add -ING
    lie → lying             die → dying
    tie → tying

ONE SYLLABLE CVC: Double final consonant, add -ING
    run → running           sit → sitting
    stop → stopping         swim → swimming
    cut → cutting           get → getting
    hit → hitting           plan → planning

TWO+ SYLLABLES (stress on last): Double final consonant
    begin → beginning       prefer → preferring
    admit → admitting       occur → occurring

TWO+ SYLLABLES (stress NOT on last): Just add -ING
    open → opening          listen → listening
    happen → happening      visit → visiting

================================================================================
                    TIME EXPRESSIONS (Expresiones de tiempo)
================================================================================

NOW                     → ahora
RIGHT NOW               → ahora mismo
AT THE MOMENT           → en este momento
AT PRESENT              → actualmente
CURRENTLY               → actualmente
TODAY                   → hoy
THIS WEEK/MONTH/YEAR    → esta semana/mes/año
THESE DAYS              → estos días
STILL                   → todavía
LOOK!                   → ¡mira!
LISTEN!                 → ¡escucha!

→ I'm studying English at the moment.
→ She's working from home this week.
→ They're still waiting for the bus.
→ Look! The baby is walking!

================================================================================
                    STATIVE VERBS (Verbos de estado)
================================================================================

Estos verbos normalmente NO se usan en Present Continuous:

MENTAL STATES:      know, believe, think*, understand, remember, forget,
                    realize, recognize, suppose, imagine, doubt, mean

EMOTIONS:           love, like, hate, dislike, want, need, prefer, wish

SENSES:             see*, hear*, smell*, taste*, feel*, sound, look*, 
                    seem, appear

POSSESSION:         have*, own, belong, possess, contain, consist

OTHER:              be*, cost, weigh, measure, owe, deserve, fit

* Algunos de estos verbos pueden usarse en continuous con significado diferente:

┌──────────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ STATIVE (Present Simple)             │ ACTION (Present Continuous)            │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I think it's a good idea.            │ I'm thinking about the problem.        │
│ (opinion)                            │ (mental process)                       │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ I see what you mean.                 │ I'm seeing the doctor tomorrow.        │
│ (understand)                         │ (meeting)                              │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ She has two cars.                    │ She's having lunch.                    │
│ (possession)                         │ (eating)                               │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ The soup tastes delicious.           │ I'm tasting the soup.                  │
│ (has a taste)                        │ (action of tasting)                    │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ You look tired.                      │ I'm looking at you.                    │
│ (appearance)                         │ (action of looking)                    │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ He's a friendly person.              │ He's being very rude today.            │
│ (permanent characteristic)           │ (temporary behavior)                   │
└──────────────────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

================================================================================
                    PRESENT SIMPLE vs PRESENT CONTINUOUS
================================================================================

┌──────────────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PRESENT SIMPLE                       │ PRESENT CONTINUOUS                     │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Habits, routines                     │ Actions happening NOW                  │
│ I drink coffee every morning.        │ I'm drinking coffee right now.         │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Permanent situations                 │ Temporary situations                   │
│ I live in Madrid.                    │ I'm living with my parents (for now).  │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Facts and truths                     │ Changing situations                    │
│ Water boils at 100°C.                │ The weather is getting colder.         │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Scheduled events                     │ Future arrangements                    │
│ The train leaves at 9.               │ I'm meeting John tomorrow.             │
├──────────────────────────────────────┼────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ always/usually/often/never           │ now/at the moment/currently            │
└──────────────────────────────────────┴────────────────────────────────────────┘

EXAMPLES TO COMPARE:

→ I work in an office. (permanent job)
→ I'm working from home today. (temporary)

→ He speaks Spanish. (ability)
→ He's speaking Spanish right now. (action now)

→ She usually drives to work. (habit)
→ She's taking the bus this week. (temporary)
