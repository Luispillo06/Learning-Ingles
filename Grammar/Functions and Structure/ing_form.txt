================================================================================
                            THE -ING FORM IN ENGLISH
                           (La Forma -ING en Inglés)
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    SPELLING RULES (Reglas de Ortografía)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BASIC RULE: Verb + ING
work → working              read → reading
play → playing              watch → watching

VERBS ENDING IN -E: Drop -E + ING
make → making               write → writing
live → living               dance → dancing
come → coming               take → taking

Exception: be → being, see → seeing, agree → agreeing

VERBS ENDING IN -IE: Change -IE to -Y + ING
lie → lying                 die → dying
tie → tying                 

VERBS ENDING IN -EE: Keep -EE + ING
see → seeing                agree → agreeing
free → freeing              flee → fleeing

ONE SYLLABLE: CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) → Double final consonant
run → running               sit → sitting
stop → stopping             swim → swimming
cut → cutting               get → getting
hit → hitting               put → putting
shop → shopping             plan → planning

Exception: verbs ending in W, X, Y → no doubling
show → showing              fix → fixing
play → playing              stay → staying

TWO+ SYLLABLES: Double if stress is on last syllable
begin → beginning           prefer → preferring
admit → admitting           occur → occurring
refer → referring           permit → permitting

No doubling if stress is NOT on last syllable:
open → opening              listen → listening
happen → happening          visit → visiting
offer → offering            enter → entering

VERBS ENDING IN -C: Add K before -ING
panic → panicking           picnic → picnicking
traffic → trafficking       mimic → mimicking

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    USES OF THE -ING FORM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

================================================================================
1. PRESENT PARTICIPLE (Participio Presente)
================================================================================

Used in CONTINUOUS TENSES:

PRESENT CONTINUOUS:
→ I am working right now.
→ She is studying English.
→ They are playing football.

PAST CONTINUOUS:
→ I was sleeping when you called.
→ She was cooking dinner.
→ They were watching TV.

FUTURE CONTINUOUS:
→ I will be waiting for you.
→ She will be traveling tomorrow.
→ They will be celebrating.

PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS:
→ I have been working all day.
→ She has been studying for hours.
→ They have been waiting since morning.

PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS:
→ I had been sleeping for hours.
→ She had been working there for years.
→ They had been living in London.

FUTURE PERFECT CONTINUOUS:
→ I will have been working for 10 years.
→ She will have been waiting for hours.

================================================================================
2. GERUND (Gerundio) - Verb as a Noun
================================================================================

AS SUBJECT:
→ Swimming is good exercise.
→ Learning English takes time.
→ Smoking is bad for health.
→ Reading improves vocabulary.

AS OBJECT:
→ I enjoy swimming.
→ She loves dancing.
→ They finished working.
→ He avoids eating junk food.

AFTER PREPOSITIONS:
→ I'm interested in learning.
→ She's good at singing.
→ He left without saying goodbye.
→ Thank you for helping me.
→ I'm tired of waiting.
→ He's afraid of flying.

AS COMPLEMENT:
→ My hobby is reading.
→ Her job is teaching.
→ The problem is finding time.

IN COMPOUND NOUNS:
→ swimming pool
→ washing machine
→ dining room
→ parking lot
→ living room
→ sleeping bag

================================================================================
3. ADJECTIVE (Adjetivo)
================================================================================

Describes the CAUSE of a feeling (what creates the emotion):

→ The movie was boring. (la película aburre)
→ The book is interesting. (el libro interesa)
→ The news is exciting. (la noticia emociona)
→ The situation is confusing. (la situación confunde)

Common -ING adjectives:
amazing         → asombroso
amusing         → divertido
annoying        → molesto
boring          → aburrido
confusing       → confuso
disappointing   → decepcionante
embarrassing    → vergonzoso
exciting        → emocionante
exhausting      → agotador
fascinating     → fascinante
frightening     → aterrador
interesting     → interesante
relaxing        → relajante
satisfying      → satisfactorio
shocking        → impactante
surprising      → sorprendente
terrifying      → aterrador
tiring          → cansador
worrying        → preocupante

-ING vs -ED:
The movie is boring. (causa aburrimiento)
I am bored. (siento aburrimiento)

The news is exciting. (causa emoción)
I am excited. (siento emoción)

================================================================================
4. REDUCED CLAUSES (Cláusulas Reducidas)
================================================================================

Instead of a full clause, use -ING for efficiency:

ACTIVE MEANING:
→ The man standing there is my father.
   (= The man who is standing there...)
→ People living in cities are busy.
   (= People who live in cities...)
→ The boy reading a book is John.
   (= The boy who is reading...)

SIMULTANEOUS ACTIONS:
→ Walking home, I saw an accident.
   (= While I was walking home...)
→ Feeling tired, she went to bed.
   (= Because she felt tired...)
→ Not knowing what to do, he asked for help.
   (= Because he didn't know...)

AFTER CONJUNCTIONS:
→ While waiting, I read a book.
→ Before leaving, say goodbye.
→ After finishing, I went home.
→ When traveling, I always take photos.

================================================================================
5. AFTER CERTAIN VERBS (Después de ciertos verbos)
================================================================================

VERBS FOLLOWED BY GERUND (-ING):

admit           → He admitted stealing the money.
avoid           → I avoid eating too much sugar.
consider        → She's considering moving abroad.
delay           → Don't delay sending the email.
deny            → He denied breaking the window.
dislike         → I dislike getting up early.
enjoy           → We enjoy traveling.
escape          → He escaped being caught.
finish          → I finished reading the book.
give up         → She gave up smoking.
imagine         → I can't imagine living there.
keep            → Keep working hard.
mind            → Do you mind waiting?
miss            → I miss seeing my friends.
postpone        → They postponed having the meeting.
practice        → Practice speaking English.
quit            → He quit smoking.
recommend       → I recommend visiting Paris.
risk            → Don't risk losing your job.
suggest         → She suggested going to the beach.
understand      → I understand feeling nervous.

VERBS FOLLOWED BY PREPOSITION + GERUND:

apologize for   → He apologized for being late.
believe in      → I believe in working hard.
blame for       → Don't blame me for trying.
concentrate on  → Focus on studying.
depend on       → Success depends on practicing.
dream of        → I dream of traveling.
feel like       → I feel like eating pizza.
insist on       → She insisted on paying.
look forward to → I look forward to seeing you.
object to       → I object to being treated badly.
succeed in      → He succeeded in passing.
think about     → I'm thinking about moving.
worry about     → Don't worry about making mistakes.

================================================================================

For sports, recreation, and activities:

go camping          → ir de camping
go climbing         → ir a escalar
go cycling          → ir en bicicleta
go dancing          → ir a bailar
go diving           → ir a bucear
go fishing          → ir a pescar
go hiking           → ir de excursión
go jogging          → ir a trotar
go running          → ir a correr
go sailing          → ir a navegar

go skating          → ir a patinar

→ I went shopping yesterday.
→ We go hiking every weekend.
→ She loves going dancing.

================================================================================
7. VERBS + ING or INFINITIVE (Different Meaning)
================================================================================

REMEMBER:
remember + -ing = recordar algo pasado
→ I remember meeting him. (Recuerdo haberlo conocido)

remember + to = recordar hacer algo futuro
→ Remember to call me. (Recuerda llamarme)

FORGET:
forget + -ing = olvidar algo pasado
→ I'll never forget visiting Paris. (Nunca olvidaré haber visitado)

forget + to = olvidar hacer algo
→ I forgot to call her. (Olvidé llamarla)

STOP:
stop + -ing = dejar de hacer
→ Stop smoking. (Deja de fumar)

stop + to = parar para hacer
→ I stopped to smoke. (Paré para fumar)

TRY:
try + -ing = intentar, experimentar
→ Try adding more salt. (Prueba añadir más sal)

try + to = hacer un esfuerzo
→ I tried to open the door. (Intenté abrir la puerta)

REGRET:
regret + -ing = arrepentirse de
→ I regret telling her. (Me arrepiento de haberle dicho)

regret + to = lamentar informar
→ I regret to inform you... (Lamento informarle...)

MEAN:
mean + -ing = significar, implicar
→ This means working harder. (Esto significa trabajar más)

mean + to = tener intención de
→ I meant to call you. (Tenía intención de llamarte)

================================================================================
8. SPECIAL STRUCTURES
================================================================================

BUSY + -ING:
→ I'm busy working.
→ She's busy cooking dinner.

WORTH + -ING:
→ This book is worth reading.
→ It's worth trying.

SPEND/WASTE TIME + -ING:
→ I spent two hours studying.
→ Don't waste time arguing.

HAVE TROUBLE/DIFFICULTY/PROBLEMS + -ING:
→ I have trouble sleeping.
→ She has difficulty understanding.
→ They have problems concentrating.

CAN'T HELP + -ING:
→ I can't help laughgo
→ She can't help worrying.

IT'S NO USE/GOOD + -ING:
→ It's no use crying.
→ It's no good complaining.

THERE'S NO + -ING:
→ There's no denying it.
→ There's no knowing what will happen.

================================================================================
                           COMMON MISTAKES
================================================================================

❌ WRONG                          ✓ CORRECT
---------                         ---------
I enjoy to swim.                  I enjoy swimming.
She avoids to eat meat.           She avoids eating meat.
I'm interesting in art.           I'm interested in art.
The movie was bored.              The movie was boring.
Thank you for help me.            Thank you for helping me.
I look forward to see you.        I look forward to seeing you.
He's good at to cook.             He's good at cooking.
I suggest to go there.            I suggest going there.
I'm used to work hard.            I'm used to working hard.
She stopped to smoke. (quit)      She stopped smoking.
