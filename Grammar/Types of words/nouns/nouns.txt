--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                         TYPES OF NOUNS (Tipos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. COMMON NOUNS (Sustantivos Comunes)
   Nombres generales de personas, lugares, cosas o ideas.
   
   → dog, city, book, love, teacher, country, car

2. PROPER NOUNS (Sustantivos Propios)
   Nombres específicos. Siempre con mayúscula.
   
   → John, London, Microsoft, Monday, January, Spain

3. CONCRETE NOUNS (Sustantivos Concretos)
   Cosas que puedes percibir con los sentidos.
   
   → table, music, flower, perfume, coffee

4. ABSTRACT NOUNS (Sustantivos Abstractos)
   Ideas, conceptos, emociones que no puedes tocar.
   
   → love, freedom, happiness, knowledge, courage, time

5. COLLECTIVE NOUNS (Sustantivos Colectivos)
   Grupos de personas, animales o cosas.
   
   → team, family, audience, flock, herd, class, group

6. COMPOUND NOUNS (Sustantivos Compuestos)
   Dos o más palabras que forman un sustantivo.
   
   → toothbrush, bedroom, mother-in-law, bus stop, ice cream

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                  COUNTABLE vs UNCOUNTABLE NOUNS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

COUNTABLE NOUNS (Contables):
Se pueden contar. Tienen singular y plural.

Singular            Plural
--------            ------
a book              books
an apple            apples
one car             three cars
a child             children

Usan: a/an, many, few, a few, several, a number of


UNCOUNTABLE NOUNS (Incontables):
No se pueden contar. No tienen plural.

→ water, milk, bread, rice, sugar, salt
→ money, information, advice, news, furniture
→ love, happiness, knowledge, music, air
→ homework, luggage, equipment, traffic

Usan: much, little, a little, some, a lot of

Para contarlos, usa unidades:
→ a glass of water, a piece of bread, a cup of coffee
→ a piece of advice, a piece of furniture, a piece of news

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                      PLURAL FORMS (Formas Plurales)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

REGULAR PLURALS (+S):

book → books              car → cars
dog → dogs                house → houses

SPELLING RULES:

-S, -SS, -SH, -CH, -X, -Z → +ES
bus → buses               class → classes
dish → dishes             watch → watches
box → boxes               quiz → quizzes

-CONSONANT + Y → -IES
baby → babies             city → cities
party → parties           country → countries

-VOWEL + Y → +S
boy → boys                day → days
key → keys                toy → toys

-F / -FE → -VES
knife → knives            wife → wives
leaf → leaves             life → lives
wolf → wolves             shelf → shelves

Excepciones: roof → roofs, chief → chiefs, cliff → cliffs

-O → +ES (algunos)
tomato → tomatoes         potato → potatoes
hero → heroes             echo → echoes

-O → +S (otros)
photo → photos            piano → pianos
radio → radios            video → videos
zoo → zoos                kilo → kilos

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    IRREGULAR PLURALS (Plurales Irregulares)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

VOWEL CHANGE:
man → men                 woman → women
foot → feet               tooth → teeth
goose → geese             mouse → mice
louse → lice

-EN ENDING:
child → children          ox → oxen

SAME FORM (Singular = Plural):
sheep → sheep             fish → fish
deer → deer               aircraft → aircraft
species → species         series → series
means → means             Swiss → Swiss

LATIN/GREEK ORIGINS:
analysis → analyses       basis → bases
crisis → crises           thesis → theses
phenomenon → phenomena    criterion → criteria
datum → data              medium → media
curriculum → curricula    memorandum → memoranda
nucleus → nuclei          stimulus → stimuli
cactus → cacti            focus → foci
fungus → fungi            radius → radii
appendix → appendices     index → indices

ALWAYS PLURAL:
scissors, glasses, jeans, pants, trousers, shorts, pajamas,
clothes, goods, stairs, thanks, congratulations

ALWAYS SINGULAR (look plural):
news, mathematics, physics, economics, politics, athletics,
gymnastics, measles, mumps, billiards, darts

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    POSSESSIVE NOUNS (Posesivos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SINGULAR NOUNS: + 'S
→ John's car (el carro de John)
→ the dog's tail (la cola del perro)
→ my mother's house (la casa de mi madre)

PLURAL NOUNS ENDING IN -S: + '
→ the students' books (los libros de los estudiantes)
→ my parents' house (la casa de mis padres)
→ the teachers' room (la sala de profesores)

IRREGULAR PLURALS: + 'S
→ the children's toys (los juguetes de los niños)
→ the men's bathroom (el baño de hombres)
→ the women's rights (los derechos de las mujeres)

NAMES ENDING IN -S: + 'S or just '
→ James's book / James' book
→ Chris's car / Chris' car

COMPOUND NOUNS: 'S on the last word
→ my mother-in-law's opinion
→ the Prime Minister's speech

JOINT POSSESSION: 'S on the last noun
→ John and Mary's house (shared house)

SEPARATE POSSESSION: 'S on each noun
→ John's and Mary's cars (different cars)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    NOUN FUNCTIONS (Funciones)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. SUBJECT (Sujeto):
   → The dog is barking.
   → Music makes me happy.

2. OBJECT (Objeto):
   Direct: → I read a book.
   Indirect: → I gave my friend a gift.

3. COMPLEMENT (Complemento):
   → She is a doctor.
   → The winner was John.

4. OBJECT OF PREPOSITION:
   → I went to the store.
   → She lives in London.

5. APPOSITIVE (Aposición):
   → My brother, a doctor, lives in Madrid.
   → Paris, the capital of France, is beautiful.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                 NOUNS AS ADJECTIVES (Sustantivos como Adjetivos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Un sustantivo puede modificar a otro sustantivo:

NOUN + NOUN                 MEANING
---------                   -------
coffee cup                  taza de café
bus stop                    parada de autobús
chicken soup                sopa de pollo
birthday party              fiesta de cumpleaños
football player             jugador de fútbol
love story                  historia de amor
school bus                  autobús escolar
car door                    puerta del carro
kitchen table               mesa de cocina
office building             edificio de oficinas

Nota: El primer sustantivo suele ir en singular.
→ a ten-dollar bill (NOT ten-dollars)
→ a five-year plan (NOT five-years)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                         COMMON MISTAKES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

❌ WRONG                          ✓ CORRECT
---------                         ---------
I need an advice.                 I need advice. / a piece of advice.
I have many informations.         I have a lot of information.
The news are good.                The news is good.
I bought a new furniture.         I bought new furniture.
She gave me good advices.         She gave me good advice.
Childrens are playing.            Children are playing.
I have two brother-in-laws.       I have two brothers-in-law.
Mens and womens                   Men and women
The sheeps are in the field.      The sheep are in the field.
