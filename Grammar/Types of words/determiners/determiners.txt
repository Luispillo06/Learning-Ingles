================================================================================
                            DETERMINERS IN ENGLISH
                           (Determinantes en Inglés)
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                          ARTICLES (Artículos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

DEFINITE ARTICLE (Artículo Definido):

THE         - el, la, los, las      → The book is on the table.
                                    → The children are playing.

Uso de THE:
- Algo específico o ya mencionado   → I saw a dog. The dog was big.
- Único en su tipo                  → The sun, the moon, the Earth
- Superlativos                      → The best, the tallest
- Ordinales                         → The first, the second
- Instrumentos musicales            → Play the piano, the guitar
- Países con plural/union           → The United States, The Netherlands
- Océanos, mares, ríos              → The Pacific, The Thames
- Cadenas montañosas                → The Alps, The Andes

NO se usa THE:
- Nombres propios                   → John, Spain, Monday
- Conceptos generales               → Love is beautiful. Dogs are loyal.
- Deportes                          → I play football.
- Comidas del día                   → I have breakfast at 8.
- Idiomas                           → I speak English.


INDEFINITE ARTICLES (Artículos Indefinidos):

A           - un, una (antes de consonante)
              → A book, a car, a house, a university (sonido /ju/)

AN          - un, una (antes de vocal)
              → An apple, an egg, an hour (h muda), an honest person

Uso de A/AN:
- Primera mención                   → I saw a movie yesterday.
- Profesiones                       → She is a doctor.
- Expresiones de cantidad           → A few, a little, a lot of
- Con "what" exclamativo            → What a beautiful day!
- Precio/velocidad por unidad       → $5 a kilo, 60 miles an hour

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    DEMONSTRATIVES (Demostrativos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

THIS        - este, esta            → This book is interesting.
                                    (cerca, singular)

THAT        - ese, esa, aquel       → That car is expensive.
                                    (lejos, singular)

THESE       - estos, estas          → These shoes are new.
                                    (cerca, plural)

THOSE       - esos, esas, aquellos  → Those mountains are beautiful.
                                    (lejos, plural)

Usos adicionales:
- THIS para tiempo presente/futuro  → This week, this Monday
- THAT para tiempo pasado           → That day, that year
- THIS para presentar personas      → This is my friend John.
- THAT para referirse a algo dicho  → That's a good idea.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                     POSSESSIVES (Posesivos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MY          - mi, mis               → My name is John.
YOUR        - tu, tus, su (formal)  → What's your name?
HIS         - su, sus (de él)       → His car is blue.
HER         - su, sus (de ella)     → Her dress is red.
ITS         - su, sus (de cosa)     → The cat licked its paw.
OUR         - nuestro/a/os/as       → Our house is big.
THEIR       - su, sus (de ellos)    → Their children are smart.

Nota: En inglés, el posesivo concuerda con el poseedor, NO con lo poseído.
      → His book (el libro de él) - Her book (el libro de ella)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                     QUANTIFIERS (Cuantificadores)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SOME        - algo de, algunos      → I have some money.
              (afirmativo)          → There are some apples.

ANY         - algo de, alguno       → Do you have any questions?
              (negativo/interrogat) → I don't have any money.

NO          - ningún, ninguno       → I have no time.
                                    → There are no chairs.

MANY        - muchos (contables)    → Many people came.
MUCH        - mucho (incontables)   → I don't have much time.

A LOT OF    - mucho/muchos          → I have a lot of friends.
LOTS OF     - mucho/muchos          → There's lots of food.

FEW         - pocos (casi ninguno)  → Few people understand this.
A FEW       - algunos (suficientes) → I have a few friends.

LITTLE      - poco (casi nada)      → There's little hope.
A LITTLE    - un poco (suficiente)  → I have a little money.

SEVERAL     - varios                → Several students passed.
ENOUGH      - suficiente            → We have enough time.
PLENTY OF   - bastante, mucho       → There's plenty of food.

ALL         - todo/todos            → All students must attend.
BOTH        - ambos                 → Both answers are correct.
EITHER      - cualquiera de dos     → Either day is fine.
NEITHER     - ninguno de dos        → Neither option works.

EACH        - cada (individual)     → Each student has a book.
EVERY       - cada (todos)          → Every day I exercise.

MOST        - la mayoría            → Most people agree.
HALF        - la mitad              → Half the cake is gone.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                NUMBERS AS DETERMINERS (Números)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

CARDINALS (Cardinales):

ONE         - uno                   → I have one brother.
TWO         - dos                   → She has two cats.
THREE       - tres                  → Three books are on the table.
FOUR        - cuatro                → Four seasons in a year.
FIVE        - cinco                 → Five fingers on each hand.
TEN         - diez                  → Ten students in class.
TWENTY      - veinte                → Twenty dollars.
HUNDRED     - cien                  → A hundred people.
THOUSAND    - mil                   → A thousand words.
MILLION     - millón                → A million dollars.

ORDINALS (Ordinales):

FIRST       - primero               → The first day of school.
SECOND      - segundo               → My second attempt.
THIRD       - tercero               → The third floor.
FOURTH      - cuarto                → The fourth of July.
FIFTH       - quinto                → The fifth element.
LAST        - último                → The last question.
NEXT        - próximo               → The next bus.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
               INTERROGATIVE DETERMINERS (Interrogativos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT        - qué                   → What time is it?
                                    → What color do you prefer?

WHICH       - cuál, qué             → Which book do you want?
              (opciones limitadas)  → Which one is yours?

WHOSE       - de quién              → Whose bag is this?
                                    → Whose turn is it?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
               DISTRIBUTIVE DETERMINERS (Distributivos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EACH        - cada (individual)     → Each child received a gift.
EVERY       - cada (totalidad)      → Every morning I jog.
EITHER      - cualquiera de dos     → Take either road.
NEITHER     - ninguno de dos        → Neither answer is correct.
ANY         - cualquier             → Any day is fine.

Diferencia EACH vs EVERY:
- EACH: enfatiza la individualidad  → Each person is unique.
- EVERY: enfatiza la totalidad      → Every person must vote.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
             EXCLAMATIVE DETERMINERS (Exclamativos)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT        - qué                   → What a beautiful day!
                                    → What lovely flowers!

SUCH        - tal, tan              → Such a nice person!
                                    → Such beautiful weather!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                    PRE-DETERMINERS (Pre-determinantes)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ALL         - todo                  → All the students passed.
BOTH        - ambos                 → Both my parents work.
HALF        - la mitad              → Half the cake is left.
DOUBLE      - doble                 → Double the amount.
TWICE       - el doble              → Twice the price.
SUCH        - tal                   → Such a good idea.
WHAT        - qué (exclamativo)     → What a surprise!
QUITE       - todo un               → Quite a story!
RATHER      - bastante              → Rather a long wait.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
                      DIFFERENCE TABLE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

| COUNTABLE (Contables)     | UNCOUNTABLE (Incontables)  |
|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| a/an apple                | (no article) water         |
| many apples               | much water                 |
| few apples                | little water               |
| a few apples              | a little water             |
| some apples               | some water                 |
| any apples                | any water                  |
| no apples                 | no water                   |
| several apples            | (not used)                 |
| a lot of apples           | a lot of water             |
| enough apples             | enough water               |

================================================================================
                           COMMON MISTAKES
================================================================================

❌ WRONG                          ✓ CORRECT
---------                         ---------
I have much friends.              I have many friends.
I don't have many money.          I don't have much money.
She is doctor.                    She is a doctor.
I like the music. (general)       I like music.
Each students must...             Each student must...
The both answers...               Both answers...
I have few friends. (positive)    I have a few friends.

================================================================================
                              ORDER OF DETERMINERS
================================================================================

Pre-determiner + Central determiner + Post-determiner + Noun

Examples:
- ALL (pre) + THE (central) + OTHER (post) + STUDENTS
- BOTH (pre) + MY (central) + FIRST (post) + ATTEMPTS
- HALF (pre) + THE (central) + REMAINING (post) + CAKE
